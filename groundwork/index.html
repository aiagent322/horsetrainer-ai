Groundwork Training

Groundwork is not a stepping stone — it is the foundation every horse needs throughout its entire career. A horse that responds correctly from the ground is safer, more trainable, and better prepared for everything that comes under saddle.

Why Groundwork Matters

Every behavioral problem under saddle has a groundwork solution — or reveals a groundwork gap. A horse that doesn't yield its hindquarters will resist a leg cue under saddle. A horse that doesn't back respectfully on the ground won't back softly with a rein. A horse that hasn't been desensitized properly will spook unpredictably on the trail.

The ground is where you earn the horse's trust, establish your leadership, and build the communication language you'll use for the rest of your training relationship. Time spent on the ground is never wasted.

The Groundwork Sequence

There is a correct order. Each skill builds on the previous one. Don't skip ahead — holes in the foundation become problems later.

1
Halter Breaking

First contact, pressure and release, accepting the halter and lead.

2
Leading & Rope Manners

Walking with, stopping, backing — the basic halter conversation.

3
Yielding Hindquarters

The single most important groundwork exercise. Creates lateral bend and respect.

4
Yielding Forequarters

Moving the front end independently. Completes the two-point yield.

5
Backing on the Ground

Soft, willing backup on light pressure — builds respect and responsiveness.

6
Desensitizing

Tarps, bags, flags, ropes, spray bottles — building confidence systematically.

7
Round Pen Fundamentals

Join-up, direction control, gait transitions without a line.

8
Lunging Basics

Circle work on the lunge — gait control, transitions, voice commands.

9
Trailer Loading

The final groundwork test. A horse that loads willingly trusts you completely.
